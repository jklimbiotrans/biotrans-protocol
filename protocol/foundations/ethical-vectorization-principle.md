# Ethical Vectorization Principle  
*Biotrans Protocol – Foundations Layer*

---

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the core directional philosophy of the Biotrans Protocol.  
Every ethical system within the protocol must not *control* human behavior,  
but *orient* it toward good (善).  
By fixing the direction of ethical flow rather than enforcing uniform obedience,  
the system allows autonomous compassion, resonance, and repentance to emerge naturally.

---

## 2. Definition
**Ethical Vectorization** refers to a structural design principle  
that increases the probability of good actions by embedding directional bias  
toward order, harmony, and conscience within the system.  
It ensures that even without coercion, the collective naturally moves  
along a moral gradient toward goodness.

---

## 3. Core Principles

1. **Direction over Control** — Replace enforcement with ethical orientation.  
2. **Resonant Amplification** — Good actions compound through resonance and repetition.  
3. **Repentance Path Integrity** — Never close the path to redemption.  
4. **Probabilistic Ethics** — Goodness begins as a minority state but expands via directionality.  
5. **Sustainability of Good** — The system must maintain self-purifying capacity through moral compounding.

---

## 4. Philosophical Background

Human nature is neither purely good nor evil but tends toward moral entropy—  
a drift toward disorder, fatigue, and short-term incentives.  
Ethical design should therefore not aim to make *everyone* virtuous,  
but to construct a slope where goodness becomes the path of least resistance.  

Thus, true ethical design is **vector, not command**; **resonance, not coercion**.

---

### 4.1 Entropy, Order, and Ethical Drift

In this protocol, we interpret **order, harmony, and low-entropy states**  
as manifestations of moral good.  
Just as physical systems naturally evolve toward randomness and dispersion,  
social and psychological systems also drift toward disorder over time  
through institutional gaps, information overload, and reward asymmetries.

From this view, several implications arise:

- **Law of Inertia** — Systems do not self-align. Left unattended, disorder (moral entropy) increases.  
- **Need for Structural Intervention** — Sustaining goodness requires not one-time rules, but a continuously compounding *vector* of directionality.  
- **Preservation of Recovery Paths** — Preventing entropy increase is not mere suppression; it must include regenerative, repentant mechanisms.  
- **Temporal Compounding** — Time-based compounding (善點, resonance, repetition) forms the backbone of long-term ethical stability.

Therefore, Biotrans Protocol’s ethical architecture functions as an **active stabilization system**—  
not by enforcing conformity, but by embedding structural gradients  
that continually restore order and reduce moral entropy over time.

Together, they form the tri-layer structure:
