## 1. Long Arc of Human Productivity

Human civilization has experienced three major productivity revolutions.  
Each revolution replaced a different “human capability” with technology.

### **① Industrial Revolution (1760–1970): Machines replaced the human body**
- Steam engines, factories, electricity, mass production  
- Technology amplified and later fully replaced physical labor  
- Manual labor ceased to be the dominant source of societal value  
- The “muscle premium” disappeared

### **② Information Revolution (1970–2020): Computers amplified the human brain**
- Semiconductors, PCs, the internet, smartphones  
- Knowledge work became the core of economic growth  
- Specialized education and professional expertise were key assets  
- The “cognitive premium” reached its historical peak

### **③ AI Revolution (2020–2035): Algorithms replace cognitive labor**
- Large language models, autonomous agents, generative AI  
- Translation, coding, diagnostics, analysis, research → automated  
- Degrees, credentials, and technical knowledge lose scarcity  
- The value of human intelligence begins a structural decline
